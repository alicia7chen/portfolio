Seattle Municipal Golf Consulting Project

There are four golf courses under the jurisdiction of Seattle Parks and Recreation: Interbay Golf Course, Jackson Municipal Golf, Jefferson Municipal Golf and West Seattle Municipal Golf Course. Fromerly managed by nonprofit organization Municipal Golf Seattle, these courses have traditionally not to use seasonal rates that would lower green fees during winter months. Compared to other golfing locations in the Seattle area who do lower their green fees in the winter, the city of Seattle's golf courses price differential from lower green fees in the summer becomes nonexistent in the winter months.

Problem Space

Should Municipal Golf Seattle alter their green fee structure? What will the outcome of matching fees with other golfing locations in the area be? Will capital improvements to the courses become profitable over time?

Insight Provided

To answer these questions, I was provided with a cross-section/time-series data set of a year's worth of golfing at golf courses in the Seattle area including: number of rounds played, fee during that month, rainfall, temperature, rating (difficulty of course), yardage of course as well as dummy variables for course features like paved cart roads, driving range availability, and whether it was a winter month (November, December, January, February) or not.

Existing Competitors

Various other country clubs, golf ranges, and venues in the Seattle area offer golfing experiences similar to Seattle Municipal Golf. After conducting competitive analysis Municipal Golf has greatest separation potential from its competitors through price points, variety of courses, and accessibility due to its unique standing as a public golfing area.

Problem Approach

To determine whether the elasticity of demand is seasonal in nature, I set up a residual demand curve at the course-level using number of rounds played and a dummy variable for whether or not it was a winter month. Using hypothesis testing to determine the effects of variables like poor weather and difficulty of ranges, there will be difficulty in separating some coinciding variables. For example, golf range workers may be more reluctant to rent out carts when it is muddy. Using R and RStudio, I was able to set up and run regressions, plot trends, and conduct hypothesis testing.

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution was for Municipal Golf to provide dynamic rates based on time of day, season and course, invest in capital improvements and embrace the lack of barriers to entry (no club membership required & open to the public) and existence as a local non-corporation run organization as the focal point of Municipal Golf Seattle's identity.
