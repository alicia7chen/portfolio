Comcast UX Research Analyst Intern

From June to August 2021 I had the opportunity to intern at Comcast NBCUniversal as a UX Research Analyst Intern. Through this internship I was exposed to both sides of qualitative and quantitative UX methods such as conducting user interviews via userzoom go, data analysis using R and RStudio, survey programming with Qualtrics, retrieving existing and relevant research from the Looker database to inform current and future research projects, design sprints, user experience workshopping and brainstorming through Miro and Freehand.


A Brief Look At What I Got To Work On!

Xfinity Connected Living: drafted and revised survey to reduce cognitive load, programmed survey in Qualtrics, facilitated customer interviews via userzoom go, created 4 user personas based on survey and interview data
Xfinity Privacy: data analysis using R and Excel macros on survey data to determine customer trust levels of chat bots, information customers are comfortable/not comfortable sharing, and preferred authentication methods
Xfinity Sports Segmentation: used k means clustering to create segments of customers based on similar traits and behaviors in relation to sports content consumption

Takeaways

One of my biggest takeaways from this internship was the breaking of the ideal . For school projects we are often asked to think about who our "ideal user" is, however in real life, many of our users don't fall into this category. Many peoples' preferences are murky without a straightforward answer one way or the other. Knowing how to navigate around ambiguity and creating the best solution possible out of uncertainty was a big area of growth for me. Learning how to cater to different groups of users, incorporate feedback from a variety of clients, internal and external, helped me develop as a better analyst and become a more sympathetic designer. Another aspect of UX that I really grew to appreciate from this internship is the little details, I remember being asked to research on whether or not the hardware team should backlight one button or not. The specificity and intricacies of the design process was something I found both surprising and endearing. The dedication and passion for creating the best experience for the users down to the most minute features was so fascinating to see in a corporate environment. The amount of thought and consideration put into these products is something I won't forget next time I pick up a remote.
