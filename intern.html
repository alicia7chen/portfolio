Comcast UX Research Analyst Intern

I received the opportunity to intern at Comcast NBCUniversal as a UX Research Analyst Intern. Through this internship I was exposed to both sides of qualitative and quantitative UX methods such as conducting user interviews via userzoom go, data analysis using R and RStudio, survey programming with Qualtrics, retrieving existing and relevant research from the Looker database to inform current and future research projects and design sprints, user experience workshopping and brainstorming through Miro and Freehand.

  • Xfinity Connected Living: drafted and revised survey to reduce cognitive load, programmed survey in Qualtrics, facilitated customer interviews via userzoom go, created 4 user personas based on survey and interview data

  • Xfinity Privacy: data analysis using R and Excel macros on survey data to determine customer trust levels of chat bots, information customers are comfortable/not comfortable sharing, and preferred authentication methods

  • Xfinity Sports Segmentation: used k means clustering to create segments of customers based on similar traits and behaviors in relation to sports content consumption

    • One of my biggest takeaways from this internship was the breaking of the ideal . For school projects we are often asked to think about who our ideal user is, however in real life, many of our users don't fall into this category. Learning how to cater to different groups of users, incorporate feedback from a variety of clients, internal and external, helped me develop as a better analyst and become a more sympathetic designer.
